Zurich's water fountains

With around 1200 drinking fountains, Zurich is truly a city of water! Besides the fountains' historical and architectural heritage that enrich the city, some of the fountains provide water with a much higher mineral content than the tap water in Zurich. These fountains are cherished by the residents of Zurich, who sometimes collect the acclaimed water from these fountains in bottles for drinking at home.
The secret behind these special fountains is that Zurich actually has two drinking water networks. The first is the normal tap water network that distributes water to households and businesses, as well as most of the water fountains of Zurich. The water for this network comes from the lake, from springs, and from the aquifer. The second network is a spring water network that feeds around 400 fountains, most of them of historic importance in Zurich's old town. The water for this network comes from around 160 springs in the city of Zurich and around 120 springs in the Sihl and Lorze valleys, around 20 km away from the city

About the project

This map is the first step of an Open-Source web application for discovering Zurich’s (and later the World’s) fountains, promoting and supporting both Open Data and Open Source Software.

The background map was designed using Mapbox based on Open Street Map data and is displayed with theLeaflet JavaScript library. The water fountain data was downloaded from data.stadt-zuerich.ch on 30.09.2017.

We hope to be able to further develop this map so that citizens and visitors in Zurich can fully appreciate the extensive water fountain network provided by the city. Our vision is that this map not only lets people know where to find water in times of disaster, but also raises awareness about Zurich's historic fountains, and encourages people to drink more of the delicious water!
